    Fall 2020

    COMP 484-01: Intro to AI

    Project: Painting for the Lazy: Neural Style Transfer Exploration and Comparison

    For this team project, three of my fellow students and I implemented and modified an NST script in a Google Colab notebook based off of previous work performed by Yuan (2018). We used the VGG-19 model and custom gradient descent functions to compare different optimizers and intermediate layers of the VGG-19 model. This entailed first preprocessing the input images through scaling, normalization, and resizing the arrays to be compatible with the batch processing utilized by Eager Execution. Then, we could quantitatively and qualitatively analyze which optimizers and layers performed best.

    Personal Projects: Recipe Ventures

    Recipe Ventures is a recipe look-up Android app. It prompts a user to enter any snack/meal/food of interest and then adds it to a home screen list. A user can then click on the given input and a specific recipe with a title, photo, and instructions will appear. If the recipe is unsatisfactory, a user can click to find another recipe until they are happy.     Summer 2019

    Research: Expanding Blocks4All

    Clare Boothe Luce Scholar

    For this application, I worked with a team to create an accessible version of Wonder Workshop's Blockly application. This app encourages youth to learn block-based programming, but it is not accessible for low vision and blind students. My Professor Lauren R. Milne began the process of creating Blocks4All as a graduate student. My role this summer was to expand the backend to enable students to save, load, create functions, and utilize variables to make the robot Dash do whatever they want. Moreover, I provided support for VoiceOver and Switch Control so low vision and blind students can more easily navigate the app. I performed this research as a Clare Boothe Luce Scholar, which was a scholarship awarded to eight students at Macalester College in 2019.
